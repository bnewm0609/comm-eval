Describing Colors in Context

Study Information

  • In this study we want to see how people describe colors to others.
  • Your task will be to play a color identification game. In this game:
    • Two players, a listener and a speaker are shown the same three colors.
    • The speaker sees one of the colors as the target and describes this target color to the listener.
    • At first, the listener does not know which color is the target, and uses the speaker's description to try to identify the target.
  • You will be the speaker for this game. The target color you will be describing is set aside to the left, surrounded by a black box.
  • The speaker and listener will likely see the colors in different orders, so please do not use spatial descriptions like "left" or "right".
  • There are two parts to this study. In each part, you will play a version of the color description game 5 times. Specific instructions are at the beginning of each part.

Part 1 of 2

Instructions

Describe the target so that the listener can pick it out from the other colors. The first game is completed for you as an example.
